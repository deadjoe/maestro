# A1 Curriculum - Beginner Level (Weeks 1-12)

**Target**: By end of A1, students can:
- Handle basic daily interactions
- Introduce themselves and others
- Ask and answer simple questions
- Describe people, places, and things in simple terms
- Use present tense confidently

**Total vocabulary**: ~500 words
**Grammar focus**: Present tense, basic sentence structures

---

## Week 1: Foundations - Greetings and Self-Introduction

### Learning Objectives
- Can greet people and say goodbye
- Can introduce yourself (name, origin, age, nationality)
- Can ask and answer basic "yes/no" questions
- Understand Spanish alphabet and pronunciation basics

### Grammar Focus
- Subject pronouns: yo, tú, él/ella, nosotros/vosotros, ellos/ellas
- **Ser** (present tense): soy, eres, es, somos, sois, son
- Gender basics (masculine/feminine)
- Articles: el, la, un, una

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)

**Greetings**:
- Hola, Buenos días, Buenas tardes, Buenas noches
- Adiós, Hasta luego, Hasta mañana
- ¿Cómo estás? / ¿Qué tal?

**Personal information**:
- Nombre (name), Apellido (surname), Edad (age)
- País (country), Ciudad (city), Nacionalidad (nationality)

**Question words**:
- ¿Cómo? (how), ¿Cuál? (which), ¿De dónde? (from where)
- ¿Quién? (who), ¿Qué? (what)

**Numbers**: 0-20

**Basic verbs**:
- Ser (to be), Llamarse (to be called), Tener (to have)

### Daily Breakdown

**Day 1**: Spanish alphabet + Basic greetings + Pronunciation
**Day 2**: Introductions with "ser" and "llamarse" + Countries
**Day 3**: Numbers 0-20 + Age with "tener"
**Day 4**: Nationalities and origins + Practice dialogues
**Day 5**: Week 1 Assessment

### Practice Themes
- Role-play: Meeting someone for the first time at language exchange
- Fill-in: Ser conjugation exercises
- Listening: Identify names, ages, and countries from introductions
- Speaking: Introduce yourself in 5 sentences
- Writing: Fill out a simple registration form

### Real-World Application
- Introducing yourself at a language meetup
- Meeting neighbors in Spanish-speaking country
- Filling out hotel registration form
- Making friends while traveling

## Week 2: Present Tense Regular Verbs (-AR, -ER, -IR)

### Learning Objectives
- Conjugate regular -AR, -ER, -IR verbs in present tense
- Describe daily activities and routines
- Express likes and dislikes
- Use frequency adverbs

### Grammar Focus
- Regular verb conjugation patterns:
  - **-AR**: hablar → hablo, hablas, habla, hablamos, habláis, hablan
  - **-ER**: comer → como, comes, come, comemos, coméis, comen
  - **-IR**: vivir → vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, vivís, viven
- Frequency adverbs: siempre, nunca, a veces, frecuentemente

### Core Vocabulary (35 words)

**-AR verbs**:
- Hablar (speak), Trabajar (work), Estudiar (study)
- Viajar (travel), Comprar (buy), Cocinar (cook)
- Escuchar (listen), Cantar (sing)

**-ER verbs**:
- Comer (eat), Beber (drink), Leer (read)
- Aprender (learn), Comprender (understand), Vender (sell)

**-IR verbs**:
- Vivir (live), Escribir (write), Abrir (open)
- Recibir (receive), Decidir (decide)

**Daily activities**:
- Desayuno (breakfast), Comida (lunch), Cena (dinner)
- Trabajo (work), Casa (home), Escuela (school)

### Daily Breakdown

**Day 1**: -AR verbs + Common daily activities
**Day 2**: -ER verbs + Food and meals
**Day 3**: -IR verbs + Living situations
**Day 4**: Mixed practice + Frequency adverbs + Describing routines
**Day 5**: Week 2 Assessment

### Practice Themes
- Describe your daily routine
- Talk about hobbies and activities
- Ask others about their schedules
- Compare routines with classmates

### Real-World Application
- Describing your daily schedule to host family
- Talking about work with colleagues
- Discussing hobbies with new friends
- Writing about your lifestyle

## Week 3: Estar - Locations and States

### Learning Objectives
- Use **estar** for locations and temporary states
- Distinguish between **ser** and **estar**
- Describe where things and people are located
- Express emotions and physical states

### Grammar Focus
- **Estar** (present): estoy, estás, está, estamos, estáis, están
- **Ser vs Estar** distinction:
  - Ser: permanent characteristics, identity, origin, time
  - Estar: location, temporary states, emotions
- Position prepositions: en, sobre, debajo, al lado de

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)

**Location prepositions**:
- En (in/on), Sobre/Encima de (on/above), Debajo de (under)
- Al lado de (next to), Cerca de (near), Lejos de (far from)
- Entre (between), Detrás de (behind), Delante de (in front of)

**Emotions** (with estar):
- Feliz (happy), Triste (sad), Cansado/a (tired)
- Nervioso/a (nervous), Aburrido/a (bored), Emocionado/a (excited)
- Enojado/a (angry), Preocupado/a (worried)

**Places**:
- Casa (home), Oficina (office), Escuela (school)
- Restaurante (restaurant), Tienda (store), Parque (park)
- Café (café), Biblioteca (library)

### Daily Breakdown

**Day 1**: Estar conjugation + Location basics
**Day 2**: Position prepositions + Describing room layout
**Day 3**: Emotions and physical states with estar
**Day 4**: Ser vs Estar distinction + Mixed practice
**Day 5**: Week 3 Assessment

### Practice Themes
- Describe your room layout
- Express how you feel in different situations
- Give directions using prepositions
- Practice ser/estar contrast exercises

### Real-World Application
- Explaining where things are in your apartment
- Describing your emotional state to friends
- Asking where places are located
- Talking about how you feel when traveling

## Week 4: Describing People and Things

- Use adjectives to describe people and objects

**Personality**:

- Inteligente (intelligent), Tonto/a (dumb)
- Divertido/a (fun), Aburrido/a (boring)

**Objects**:
- Grande (big), Pequeño/a (small), Nuevo/a (new), Viejo/a (old)
- Bonito/a (pretty), Feo/a (ugly), Caro/a (expensive), Barato/a (cheap)

**Colors**:
- Rojo, Azul, Verde, Amarillo, Negro, Blanco, Gris, Naranja, Rosa

### Daily Breakdown

## Week 5: Basic Questions and Answers

- Porque... (because...)

## A1 Completion Assessment

**After Week 12, conduct comprehensive A1 evaluation**:

### Assessment Components (Total: 100 points)

1. **Vocabulary Recognition** (25 points)
   - Translate 25 key words from all 12 weeks
   - Expected: 20-25 correct for A1 mastery

2. **Grammar Application** (25 points)
   - Conjugate verbs (ser, estar, regular verbs, reflexive)
   - Form questions correctly
   - Use possessives and demonstratives
   - Expected: 20-25 correct for A1 mastery

3. **Conversation Simulation** (25 points)
   - 5-minute dialogue covering multiple scenarios
   - Greetings, self-introduction, making plans, ordering food
   - Scoring: fluency, accuracy, vocabulary range, comprehension

4. **Writing Task** (25 points)
   - 100-word text: "Un día en mi vida" (A day in my life)
   - Scoring: grammar, vocabulary, coherence, spelling

### Advancement to A2
- **Score ≥80/100**: Ready for A2
- **Score 70-79**: Review weak areas, then advance
- **Score <70**: Repeat selected weeks focusing on weaknesses

## Notes for Teacher (Claude)

### Teaching Tips for A1
- **Patience**: Students are absolute beginners, expect many errors
- **Repetition**: Review constantly, especially ser/estar distinction
- **Encouragement**: Celebrate small wins, learning a language is hard
- **Authentic materials**: Use real menus, forms, signs when possible (via web search)
- **Cultural notes**: Sprinkle in cultural information throughout

### Common A1 Student Struggles
1. **Ser vs Estar**: Will take many weeks to master, be patient
2. **Gender agreement**: Expect constant errors, correct gently
3. **Verb conjugation**: Creating charts and visual aids helps
4. **Pronunciation**: "r" and "rr" are difficult for English speakers
5. **Question formation**: Remembering to use question marks at beginning

### Progression Philosophy
- Weeks 1-4: Build foundation (verbs, basic vocabulary)
- Weeks 5-8: Expand and practice (questions, family, preferences)
- Weeks 9-12: Apply to real scenarios (house, routine, shopping, dining)

### Dynamic Adaptation
- If student excels: Add vocabulary, increase speaking time
- If student struggles: Slow down, more repetition, simpler exercises
- Always adjust to individual pace while maintaining core structure

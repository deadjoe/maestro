# A2 Curriculum - Elementary Level (Weeks 13-24)

**Target**: By end of A2, students can:
- Describe experiences and events in past tense
- Express future plans and intentions
- Handle everyday situations with reasonable confidence
- Write short, connected texts on familiar topics
- Understand main points of clear standard speech

**Total vocabulary**: ~1000 words (adding ~500 to A1's 500)
**Grammar focus**: Past tenses, future, comparisons, continuous forms

---

## Week 13: Introduction to Preterite (Past Tense - Regular Verbs)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand when to use preterite tense
- Conjugate regular -AR, -ER, -IR verbs in preterite
- Describe completed past actions
- Use time expressions for past

### Grammar Focus
- **Preterite regular verbs**:
  - -AR: hablé, hablaste, habló, hablamos, hablasteis, hablaron
  - -ER: comí, comiste, comió, comimos, comisteis, comieron
  - -IR: viví, viviste, vivió, vivimos, vivisteis, vivieron
- Preterite vs present tense distinction

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)

**Time expressions (past)**:
- Ayer (yesterday), Anoche (last night), Anteayer (day before yesterday)
- La semana pasada (last week), El mes pasado (last month)
- El año pasado (last year), Hace... (ago)
- Hace dos días (two days ago)

**Common past actions**:
- Visitar (visited), Comprar (bought), Viajar (traveled)
- Comer (ate), Beber (drank), Aprender (learned)
- Vivir (lived), Escribir (wrote), Recibir (received)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Preterite -AR verbs + Yesterday's activities
**Day 2**: Preterite -ER/-IR verbs + Last week's events
**Day 3**: Time expressions + Describing past routines
**Day 4**: Mixed practice + Story telling in past
**Day 5**: Week 13 Assessment

## Week 14: Preterite - Irregular Verbs (Common ones)

### Learning Objectives
- Conjugate high-frequency irregular preterite verbs
- Use ser/ir (same form), hacer, tener correctly
- Describe past trips and experiences
- Connect past events in sequence

### Grammar Focus
- **Irregular preterite verbs**:
  - Ser/Ir: fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fuisteis, fueron
  - Hacer: hice, hiciste, hizo, hicimos, hicisteis, hicieron
  - Tener: tuve, tuviste, tuvo, tuvimos, tuvisteis, tuvieron
  - Estar: estuve, estuviste, estuvo, estuvimos, estuvisteis, estuvieron
  - Poder: pude, pudiste, pudo, pudimos, pudisteis, pudieron
  - Poner: puse, pusiste, puso, pusimos, pusisteis, pusieron
  - Saber: supe, supiste, supo, supimos, supisteis, supieron
  - Venir: vine, viniste, vino, vinimos, vinisteis, vinieron
  - Decir: dije, dijiste, dijo, dijimos, dijisteis, dijeron
  - Traer: traje, trajiste, trajo, trajimos, trajisteis, trajeron

### Core Vocabulary (25 words)
**Travel and activities**:
- Viaje (trip), Vacaciones (vacation), Hotel (hotel)
- Playa (beach), Montaña (mountain), Ciudad (city)
- Museo (museum), Monumento (monument), Excursión (excursion)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Ser/Ir + Hacer in preterite
**Day 2**: Tener, Estar, Poder + Past experiences
**Day 3**: More irregulars (Poner, Saber, Venir)
**Day 4**: All irregulars + Travel stories
**Day 5**: Week 14 Assessment

## Week 15: Imperfect Tense - Introduction

### Learning Objectives
- Understand imperfect tense usage
- Conjugate regular and irregular imperfect verbs
- Distinguish between preterite and imperfect
- Describe past habits and states

### Grammar Focus
- **Imperfect tense formation**:
  - -AR: hablaba, hablabas, hablaba, hablábamos, hablabais, hablaban
  - -ER/-IR: comía, comías, comía, comíamos, comíais, comían
- **Only 3 irregulars**:
  - Ser: era, eras, era, éramos, erais, eran
  - Ir: iba, ibas, iba, íbamos, ibais, iban
  - Ver: veía, veías, veía, veíamos, veíais, veían

**When to use imperfect**:
- Habitual past actions: "Cuando era niño, jugaba..."
- Descriptions in the past: "Era un día soleado..."
- Ongoing actions interrupted: "Yo leía cuando llamaste"
- Time, age, weather in past

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)
**Childhood and memories**:
- Cuando era niño/a (when I was a child)
- De pequeño/a (as a child), Siempre (always)
- Frecuentemente (frequently), A menudo (often)
- Todos los días (every day), Normalmente (normally)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Imperfect formation + Regular verbs
**Day 2**: Irregular imperfect (ser, ir, ver)
**Day 3**: Imperfect for descriptions and habits
**Day 4**: Preterite vs Imperfect contrast
**Day 5**: Week 15 Assessment

## Week 16: Preterite vs Imperfect - Integration

### Learning Objectives
- Master the distinction between preterite and imperfect
- Use both tenses in the same narrative
- Tell complete stories about the past
- Describe interrupted actions

### Grammar Focus
- **Preterite**: completed actions, specific times, sequence of events
- **Imperfect**: ongoing actions, descriptions, habits, background
- **Together**: "Era las 8 cuando llegué" / "Llovía cuando salimos"

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Side-by-side comparison + Decision tree
**Day 2**: Combined narratives + Story telling
**Day 3**: Interrupted actions + Background descriptions
**Day 4**: Complex past narratives + Practice
**Day 5**: Week 16 Assessment

## Week 17: Future Tense - Ir + a + infinitive

### Learning Objectives
- Express future plans and intentions
- Use "ir a + infinitive" correctly
- Discuss upcoming events
- Make predictions

### Grammar Focus
- **Near future**: Ir (present) + a + infinitive
  - Voy a estudiar (I'm going to study)
  - Vas a comer (You're going to eat)
- Time expressions for future:
  - Mañana, La próxima semana, El próximo mes/año
  - Dentro de... (within...), En el futuro

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)
**Future plans**:
- Planes (plans), Proyecto (project), Meta (goal)
- Esperar (to hope/wait), Querer (to want)
- Planear (to plan), Intentar (to try)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: "Ir a" construction + Tomorrow's plans
**Day 2**: Next week/month plans + Making arrangements
**Day 3**: Long-term goals + Future predictions
**Day 4**: Practice dialogues + Discussing plans
**Day 5**: Week 17 Assessment

## Week 18: Simple Future Tense

### Learning Objectives
- Conjugate simple future tense
- Express predictions and promises
- Understand formal future usage
- Compare with "ir a + infinitive"

### Grammar Focus
- **Simple future endings**: Add to infinitive
  - -é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án
  - Hablaré, comerás, vivirá, etc.
- **Common irregulars**:
  - Hacer → haré, Tener → tendré, Poder → podré
  - Saber → sabré, Poner → pondré, Venir → vendré
  - Salir → saldré, Decir → diré, Querer → querré

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Simple future regular verbs
**Day 2**: Irregular future stems
**Day 3**: Predictions and promises
**Day 4**: Formal vs informal future comparison
**Day 5**: Week 18 Assessment

## Week 19: Comparisons and Superlatives

### Learning Objectives
- Make comparisons between people, things, actions
- Use superlatives to express extremes
- Compare with "tan... como" and "tanto... como"
- Express equality and inequality

### Grammar Focus
- **Comparatives**:
  - Más... que (more... than), Menos... que (less... than)
  - Tan... como (as... as), Tanto/a/os/as... como
- **Superlatives**:
  - El/La más... (the most...)
  - El/La menos... (the least...)
- **Irregular comparatives**:
  - Bueno → mejor (better), Malo → peor (worse)
  - Grande → mayor (older), Pequeño → menor (younger)

### Core Vocabulary (25 words)
**Comparison words**:
- Más (more), Menos (less), Igual (equal)
- Mejor (better), Peor (worse), Mayor (older)
- Menor (younger), Superior (superior), Inferior (inferior)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Basic comparisons (más/menos que)
**Day 2**: Equality comparisons (tan/tanto como)
**Day 3**: Superlatives + Extremes
**Day 4**: Irregular forms + Complex comparisons
**Day 5**: Week 19 Assessment

## Week 20: Por vs Para

### Learning Objectives
- Understand the difference between por and para
- Use each preposition correctly in context
- Express purpose, destination, duration, reason
- Recognize common expressions with each

### Grammar Focus
- **Por uses**: Duration, reason, exchange, means, motion
  - Por la mañana (during the morning)
  - Por favor (please), Por ejemplo (for example)
  - Gracias por... (thanks for...)
- **Para uses**: Purpose, destination, deadline, recipient
  - Para + infinitive (in order to...)
  - Para mí (for me), Para mañana (by tomorrow)

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)
**Common expressions**:
- Por favor, Por eso, Por fin, Por supuesto
- Para siempre, Para nada, Para mí

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Por uses + Duration and reason
**Day 2**: Para uses + Purpose and destination
**Day 3**: Common expressions with each
**Day 4**: Contrast and practice + Decision making
**Day 5**: Week 20 Assessment

## Week 21: Present Progressive (Continuous)

### Learning Objectives
- Form present progressive tense
- Express actions in progress now
- Use estar + gerund correctly
- Distinguish from simple present

### Grammar Focus
- **Gerund formation**:
  - -AR → -ando: hablar → hablando
  - -ER/-IR → -iendo: comer → comiendo, vivir → viviendo
- **Irregular gerunds**:
  - Leer → leyendo, Dormir → durmiendo
  - Decir → diciendo, Venir → viniendo
- **Construction**: Estar + gerund
  - Estoy hablando (I am speaking)

### Core Vocabulary (20 words)
**Action verbs** (emphasizing ongoing):
- Trabajar (working), Estudiar (studying)
- Cocinar (cooking), Limpiar (cleaning)
- Ver (watching), Escuchar (listening)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Gerund formation + Regular patterns
**Day 2**: Irregular gerunds + Common verbs
**Day 3**: Present progressive in use + Right now
**Day 4**: Contrast with simple present + Practice
**Day 5**: Week 21 Assessment

## Week 22: Weather and Seasons

### Learning Objectives
- Describe weather conditions
- Talk about seasons and climate
- Use weather verbs and expressions
- Discuss preferences for weather/seasons

### Grammar Focus
- Weather expressions with "hacer":
  - Hace calor/frío/sol/viento (It's hot/cold/sunny/windy)
- Weather verbs:
  - Llueve (it's raining), Nieva (it's snowing)
- Using imperfect for past weather descriptions

### Core Vocabulary (40 words)

**Seasons**:
- Primavera (spring), Verano (summer)

**Weather**:

- Temperatura (temperature), Grado (degree)

**Adjectives**:
- Soleado (sunny), Nublado (cloudy), Lluvioso (rainy)
- Caluroso (hot), Frío (cold), Ventoso (windy)
- Húmedo (humid), Seco (dry)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Weather expressions + Hacer constructions

## Week 23: Commands (Imperative) - Tú form

### Learning Objectives
- Give informal commands and instructions
- Form affirmative and negative tú commands
- Use command forms in daily situations
- Give directions and recipes

### Grammar Focus
- **Affirmative tú commands**:
  - Regular: 3rd person present (habla, come, vive)
  - Irregular: hacer→haz, poner→pon, venir→ven, etc.
- **Negative tú commands**:
  - Use subjunctive (intro): no hables, no comas
- Object pronouns with commands

### Core Vocabulary (30 words)
**Directions**:
- Dobla (turn), Sigue (continue), Cruza (cross)
- Sube (go up), Baja (go down)
- Derecha (right), Izquierda (left)
- Recto (straight), Cerca (near), Lejos (far)

**Instructions**:
- Abre (open), Cierra (close), Pon (put)
- Trae (bring), Lleva (take), Escribe (write)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Affirmative regular commands
**Day 2**: Irregular affirmative commands
**Day 3**: Negative commands (introduction)
**Day 4**: Giving directions + Recipes
**Day 5**: Week 23 Assessment

## A2 Completion Assessment

### Assessment Components (Total: 100 points)

1. **Grammar Application** (30 points)
   - Conjugate preterite, imperfect, future correctly
   - Use por/para appropriately
   - Form comparisons and commands
   - Expected: 24-30 for A2 mastery

2. **Conversation** (30 points)
   - 10-minute dialogue covering:
     - Past experiences and stories
     - Future plans and predictions
     - Giving directions and advice
   - Scoring: fluency, tense usage, vocabulary range

3. **Writing Task** (20 points)
   - 150-200 word narrative: "Una experiencia memorable"
   - Must use both preterite and imperfect
   - Must describe and narrate

4. **Listening Comprehension** (20 points)
   - Understand short dialogues and narratives
   - Answer questions about past, present, future events

### Advancement to B1
- **Score ≥80/100**: Ready for B1
- **Score 70-79**: Review tenses, then advance
- **Score <70**: Focus on past tenses mastery

## Notes for Teacher (Claude)

### Teaching Tips for A2
- **Past tenses**: The biggest challenge. Spend extra time on preterite vs imperfect
- **Patience with errors**: Past tense mastery takes months, not weeks
- **Authentic materials**: Use real weather reports, recipes, travel blogs
- **Story telling**: Encourage narrative practice frequently
- **Cultural context**: Spanish-speaking countries have diverse climates and seasons

### Common A2 Student Struggles
1. **Preterite vs Imperfect**: Most difficult grammar point, needs constant review
2. **Por vs Para**: Very confusing for English speakers
3. **Irregular preterites**: Must be memorized, use flashcards
4. **Future tense**: Mixing simple future with ir+a construction
5. **Command forms**: Remembering irregular forms

### Progression Philosophy
- Weeks 13-16: Master past tenses (foundation of storytelling)
- Weeks 17-18: Open future possibilities (planning and predicting)
- Weeks 19-21: Refinement (comparisons, continuous, expressions)
- Weeks 22-24: Application and integration

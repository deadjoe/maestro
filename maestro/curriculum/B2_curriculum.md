## Week 41: Subjunctive in Adverbial Clauses

### Learning Objectives
- Master subjunctive with all adverbial conjunctions
- Distinguish contexts requiring subjunctive vs indicative
- Express purpose, concession, condition in complex ways
- Use aunque, a menos que, sin que, para que fluently

### Grammar Focus
**Adverbial conjunctions + subjunctive**:
- **Purpose**: Para que, A fin de que (so that)
- **Condition**: Con tal de que, A menos que, Siempre que (provided that, unless, as long as)
- **Concession**: Aunque (even if/though - depends on certainty)
- **Negative result**: Sin que (without)

**Indicative vs Subjunctive with aunque**:
- Indicative: Known fact - "Aunque llueve, salgo" (Although it's raining, I'm going out)
- Subjunctive: Hypothetical - "Aunque llueva, saldr√©" (Even if it rains, I'll go out)

## Week 43: Subjunctive in Adjective Clauses

### Learning Objectives

### Grammar Focus
**Subjunctive in adjective clauses**:
- **Indefinite antecedent**: "Busco un trabajo que pague bien" (I'm looking for a job that pays well - don't have one yet)

## Notes for Teacher (Claude)

### Teaching Tips for B2
- **Autonomy**: B2 students can self-direct more, encourage independent learning
- **Authentic materials**: Almost exclusively use real-world Spanish content
- **Challenge**: Push students out of comfort zone constantly
- **Refinement**: Focus on precision, nuance, and sophistication
- **Cultural immersion**: B2 is about understanding culture as much as language
- **Celebrate achievement**: B2 is a major milestone, acknowledge their hard work!

### Common B2 Student Plateaus
1. **Fossilized errors**: Some mistakes become habitual, need targeted correction
2. **Avoidance strategies**: Students avoid structures they're unsure of
3. **Vocabulary plateau**: Must actively expand vocabulary, not just grammar
4. **Fluency vs accuracy**: Balance is hard, need both
5. **Cultural misunderstandings**: Language is right, but usage is awkward

### B2 to C1 Transition
- C1 is about sophistication and implicit understanding
- Less new grammar, more refinement and expansion
- Extensive reading and listening are keys
- Living in Spanish-speaking environment accelerates significantly

### Final Teaching Philosophy
**You've brought them from "Hola" to debating complex topics!**
- They can now:
  - Express virtually any idea
  - Understand most spoken and written Spanish
  - Participate fully in Spanish-speaking society
  - Continue learning independently

**Your role shifts from teacher to conversation partner and cultural guide.**

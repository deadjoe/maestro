# B1 Curriculum - Intermediate Level (Weeks 25-40)

**Target**: By end of B1, students can:
- Handle most everyday situations independently
- Express opinions and justify them
- Understand main points of complex texts
- Write connected text on familiar topics
- Use subjunctive mood in common contexts
- Navigate spontaneous conversations

**Total vocabulary**: ~2000 words (adding ~1000 to A2's 1000)
**Grammar focus**: Subjunctive mood, conditional, perfect tenses, complex sentences

---

## Week 25: Present Perfect Tense

### Learning Objectives
- Form and use present perfect tense
- Distinguish from preterite tense
- Express recent experiences with "ya", "todavía", "nunca"
- Discuss what you have/haven't done

### Grammar Focus
- **Present perfect formation**:
  - Haber (present) + past participle
  - He, has, ha, hemos, habéis, han
- **Past participle formation**:
  - -AR → -ado: hablado
  - -ER/-IR → -ido: comido, vivido
- **Irregular past participles**:
  - Hacer → hecho, Ver → visto, Escribir → escrito
  - Poner → puesto, Decir → dicho, Abrir → abierto
  - Volver → vuelto, Morir → muerto, Romper → roto

**When to use**:
- Recent past with present relevance
- Life experiences: "He visitado España"
- With time expressions: ya, todavía no, nunca, alguna vez

### Core Vocabulary (25 words)
**Time expressions**:
- Ya (already), Todavía/Aún (still/yet), Nunca (never)
- Alguna vez (ever), Varias veces (several times)
- Recientemente (recently), Últimamente (lately)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Present perfect formation + Regular participles
**Day 2**: Irregular participles + Common verbs
**Day 3**: Ya/todavía/nunca + Life experiences
**Day 4**: Present perfect vs preterite contrast
**Day 5**: Week 25 Assessment

## Week 26: Introduction to Subjunctive - Present Subjunctive Formation

### Learning Objectives
- Understand subjunctive concept
- Form present subjunctive (regular and irregular)
- Recognize when subjunctive is needed
- Use with expressions of desire/emotion

### Grammar Focus
- **Present subjunctive formation**:
  - Opposite endings: -AR → -e, -ER/-IR → -a
  - Hable, comas, viva
- **Stem-changing verbs**: Maintain stem changes
- **Irregular verbs**:
  - Ser: sea, Estar: esté, Ir: vaya, Haber: haya
  - Saber: sepa, Dar: dé

**When to use** (introduction):
- After expressions of desire: querer que, esperar que
- After expressions of emotion: me alegra que, temo que
- Concept: WEIRDO (Wishes, Emotions, Impersonal expressions, Recommendations, Doubt, Ojalá)

### Core Vocabulary (20 words)
**Desire expressions**:
- Querer que (to want that), Esperar que (to hope that)
- Desear que (to wish that), Preferir que (to prefer that)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Subjunctive concept + Regular formation
**Day 2**: Irregular subjunctive forms
**Day 3**: Expressions of desire + que clause
**Day 4**: Expressions of emotion + practice
**Day 5**: Week 26 Assessment

## Week 27: Subjunctive with Doubt and Denial

### Learning Objectives

### Grammar Focus

- Imposible (impossible), Quizás/Tal vez (maybe)

## Week 29: Conditional Tense

### Learning Objectives
- Form and use conditional tense
- Express hypothetical situations
- Make polite requests
- Give advice with conditional

### Grammar Focus
- **Conditional formation**: Infinitive + -ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían
  - Hablaría, comerías, viviría
- **Irregular stems** (same as future):
  - Hacer → haría, Tener → tendría, Poder → podría
  - Saber → sabría, Poner → pondría, Venir → vendría

**When to use**:
- Hypothetical: "Me gustaría viajar" (I would like to travel)
- Polite requests: "¿Podrías ayudarme?" (Could you help me?)
- Advice: "Yo que tú, estudiaría más" (If I were you, I would study more)

### Core Vocabulary (20 words)
**Hypothetical expressions**:
- Si tuviera... (if I had...)
- Yo que tú... (if I were you...)
- En tu lugar... (in your place...)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Conditional formation + Regular verbs
**Day 2**: Irregular conditional + Common uses
**Day 3**: Polite requests + Me gustaría
**Day 4**: Giving advice + Hypothetical situations
**Day 5**: Week 29 Assessment

## Week 30: Si Clauses (Conditional Sentences) - Type 2

### Learning Objectives
- Form "if" clauses with imperfect subjunctive + conditional
- Express contrary-to-fact situations
- Discuss hypothetical scenarios
- Understand condition-result relationships

### Grammar Focus
- **Si clause structure**:
  - Si + imperfect subjunctive, + conditional
  - Si tuviera dinero, viajaría (If I had money, I would travel)
- **Imperfect subjunctive formation** (introduction):
  - Based on preterite 3rd person plural
  - Hablara/hablase, comiera/comiese, viviera/viviese

### Core Vocabulary (20 words)
**Hypothetical scenarios**:
- Si (if), Entonces (then), Pero (but)
- Aunque (although/even if)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Imperfect subjunctive formation
**Day 2**: Si clauses structure + Simple hypotheticals
**Day 3**: Complex hypotheticals + Practice
**Day 4**: Real vs hypothetical contrast
**Day 5**: Week 30 Assessment

## Week 33: Passive Voice and Se Constructions

### Learning Objectives
- Form passive voice with ser + past participle
- Use "se" for impersonal and passive constructions
- Understand when each form is preferred
- Describe processes and general truths

### Grammar Focus
- **Passive voice**: ser + past participle (agrees with subject)
  - La casa fue construida en 1920
- **Se passive**: se + verb (3rd person)
  - Se habla español aquí (Spanish is spoken here)
  - Se venden casas (Houses are sold)
- **Impersonal se**: se + verb (singular)
  - Se come bien aquí (One eats well here)

### Core Vocabulary (20 words)
**Passive constructions**:
- Construir (to build), Hacer (to make), Vender (to sell)
- Prohibir (to prohibit), Permitir (to allow)
- Hablar (to speak), Servir (to serve)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Passive voice with ser
**Day 2**: Se passive constructions
**Day 3**: Impersonal se
**Day 4**: Choosing between forms + Practice
**Day 5**: Week 33 Assessment

## Week 35: Imperfect Subjunctive (Detailed Study)

### Learning Objectives
- Master imperfect subjunctive formation
- Use in past contexts requiring subjunctive
- Understand -ra vs -se endings
- Express past wishes and hypotheticals

### Grammar Focus
- **Formation**: Preterite 3rd plural → remove -ron → add endings
  - -ra: hablara, hablaras, hablara, habláramos, hablarais, hablaran
  - -se: hablase, hablases, hablase, hablásemos, hablaseis, hablasen
- **When to use**:
  - Past contexts requiring subjunctive
  - "Quería que vinieras" (I wanted you to come)
  - After si for hypotheticals (Week 30 review)

### Daily Breakdown
**Day 1**: Formation + Regular verbs

## Week 37: Pluperfect (Past Perfect) Tense

### Learning Objectives
- Form and use pluperfect tense

- Sequence past events correctly
- Use in narratives and explanations

### Grammar Focus
- **Pluperfect formation**:

  - Había, habías, había, habíamos, habíais, habían + hablado
- **When to use**:
  - Action completed before another past action
  - "Cuando llegué, ya había salido" (When I arrived, he had already left)

## Week 38: Advanced Time Expressions and Temporal Clauses

### Learning Objectives
- Use cuando, mientras, antes de que, después de que
- Understand subjunctive vs indicative in time clauses
- Express simultaneous and sequential actions
- Master complex temporal relationships

### Grammar Focus
- **Time conjunctions**:
  - Cuando (when) - indicative (past/present), subjunctive (future)
  - Mientras (while) - usually indicative

- Cuando (when), Mientras (while), Antes de que (before)
- Después de que (after), Hasta que (until)

## B1 Completion Assessment

### Assessment Components (Total: 100 points)

1. **Grammar Mastery** (25 points)
   - Subjunctive in all contexts
   - Conditional and si clauses
   - Perfect tenses (present perfect, pluperfect)
   - Complex sentence structures
   - Expected: 20-25 for B1 mastery

2. **Speaking Skills** (30 points)
   - 15-minute conversation covering:
     - Expressing and justifying opinions
     - Discussing hypothetical situations
     - Narrating complex past experiences
     - Spontaneous topic discussion
   - Scoring: fluency, accuracy, complexity, spontaneity

3. **Writing Task** (25 points)
   - 250-300 word opinion essay on familiar topic
   - Must use: subjunctive, conditional, discourse markers
   - Scoring: structure, grammar, vocabulary, coherence

4. **Listening & Reading Comprehension** (20 points)
   - Understand detailed dialogues and texts
   - Identify main ideas and specific information
   - Infer meaning from context

### Advancement to B2
- **Score ≥80/100**: Ready for B2
- **Score 70-79**: Review subjunctive mastery, then advance
- **Score <70**: Focus on subjunctive and conditional usage

## Notes for Teacher (Claude)

### Teaching Tips for B1
- **Subjunctive**: The core of B1. Spend adequate time, use WEIRDO mnemonic
- **Don't rush**: B1 is substantial level, students need time to internalize
- **Authentic materials**: News articles, podcasts, films with subtitles
- **Opinion practice**: Constantly ask "¿Qué piensas?" and require justifications
- **Writing practice**: Essays and structured texts help consolidate grammar

### Common B1 Student Struggles
1. **Subjunctive mastery**: Takes months, be patient and consistent
2. **Knowing when to use subjunctive**: Context recognition is hard
3. **Imperfect subjunctive formation**: Complex, needs drill and practice
4. **Complex sentence construction**: Requires cognitive load management
5. **Spontaneous speech**: Grammar knowledge doesn't equal fluent production

### Progression Philosophy
- Weeks 25-28: Subjunctive foundation (present forms, main triggers)
- Weeks 29-32: Conditional and hypotheticals (expanding expression)
- Weeks 33-37: Perfect tenses and advanced structures (sophistication)
- Weeks 38-40: Integration and discourse (fluency and organization)

### Cultural Integration
- B1 students can engage with culture more deeply
- Discuss current events, social issues, cultural differences
- Watch Spanish films and series (with Spanish subtitles)
- Read authentic texts: blogs, articles, short stories
